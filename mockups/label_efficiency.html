Pretraining is label‑efficient

Frozen features + a probe trained on a fraction of downstream labels. Our pretrained encoder reaches near‑full accuracy with a fraction of the labels a random‑init encoder needs — and with few labels already beats random with all labels.

Figure. Label efficiency of the pretrained encoder. Downstream probes are trained on {1,5,10,25,50,100}% of the training labels (frozen features; mean over subsamples) and evaluated on the full test set. Mumtaz: with a linear probe on frozen features, pretraining reaches its plateau (~0.95 BA) at 5% of labels, and even at 1% (0.91) exceeds a random encoder using 100% of labels (0.82). ISRUC: with the seq2seq head, pretraining reaches ~0.70 BA at 10% of labels — above random at 100% (0.564); below 10% both sit at chance because the sequence‑transformer head itself needs enough sequences to train (an artifact of head capacity, not feature quality). The shaded band is the pretrained‑minus‑random gap; it widens as labels shrink.
